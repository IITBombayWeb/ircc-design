Cryo High Resolution Transmission Electron Microscopy Facility
Transmission electron microscopes typically use high energy electron beams transmitted through very thin samplesin order to analyze the microstructure of materials. Electrons are accelerated at several hundred KV, resulting in wavelengths much smaller than that of light, and are focused with electromagnetic lenses for imaging. Images of samples generated are recorded on digital camera.
